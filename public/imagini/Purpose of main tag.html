what s the purpose of a main tag in html?
ChatGPT said:

The <main> tag in HTML is used to define the main content of a webpage. Here's a quick rundown of its purpose:

What it does:

  • It represents the core content that's directly related to the primary purpose of the document.

  • It helps search engines and assistive technologies (like screen readers) understand which part of the page is central.

  • There should be only one <main> tag per page.

What it shouldn't contain:

  • Repeated elements like headers, footers, sidebars, or navigation links.

  • Content that's not unique to the specific page.

Example:

html
<body>
  <header>
    <h1>My Website</h1>
  </header>

  <nav>
    <!-- navigation links -->
  </nav>

  <main>
    <h2>Welcome to my blog!</h2>
    <p>This is where the main article content goes.</p>
  </main>

  <footer>
    <!-- footer info -->
  </footer>
</body>

In short: <main> helps structure your page semantically, making it cleaner and more accessible. Want to dive deeper into accessibility or SEO aspects of it?
